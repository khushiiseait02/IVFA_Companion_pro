Types of IVF Treatments

Understanding the different types of IVF can help you make informed decisions with your healthcare provider.

1. Traditional IVF

Involves retrieving eggs, fertilizing them with sperm in a lab, and transferring an embryo into the uterus.

2. ICSI (Intracytoplasmic Sperm Injection)

A single sperm is injected directly into an egg. Often used when there are male fertility issues.

3. Natural Cycle IVF

Uses the one egg a woman naturally produces during her cycle without fertility drugs.

4. Mini IVF (Minimal Stimulation)

Lower doses of medications are used to produce fewer eggs, reducing side effects and cost.

5. Donor IVF

Uses donor eggs or sperm for fertilization, often for those with infertility or genetic concerns.

6. Frozen Embryo Transfer (FET)

Embryos are frozen after IVF and transferred in a later cycle for more flexibility.
